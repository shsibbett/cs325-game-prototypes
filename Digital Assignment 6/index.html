Manhunt

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    • This is a two-player game that requires each player to try and outwit the other.
    • Both players get a random number of moves that they can use to move across the board.
    • They can also jump over certain obstacles (currently only rocks).
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    • This prototype utilizes a player switching mechanic that switches control to the other player when they run out of moves or (in the Hunted's case) if they run out of time during their turn.
    • As the Hunted could win by simply stalling on their turn, they have 5 seconds from the time they made their last move to make another move before control switches to the Monster.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    • I learned how to make a turn-based system.
    • The easiest part was coding the basic movement from tile to tile.
    • The hardest part was coding when the player's sprites would be allowed to jump over rocks. There were multiple cases that I had to account for.

    • A hiker who has become lost in the woods is hunted by a blood thirsty monster and must outrun it before it eats him.

    • The Hunted moves by using the arrow keys while the Monster moves with WASD.
    • An element of randomness is the number of moves each player gets per turn.
    • The genre is turn-based strategy.
    • The playstyle is that the players must utilize their moves efficiently to be able to outrun/eat the other player.

  • Gameplay: A paragraph describing the actions the player can perform, the system dynamics, and the core mechanic. Include a concise explanation of the prototype’s inputs and their expected effects (how to play). You can also describe game play that is not in the prototype. You may include mock-up images for parts of the game not in the prototype.
    • This game requires two players: a Hiker being Hunted, and a Monster.
    • It is the Monster's goal to catch up with and eat the Hunted; it is the Hunted's goal to survive until the time limit is up.
    • The Hunted always goes first and moves with the arrow keys.
    • The Monster moves with WASD.
    • On each players' turn, they recieve a random number of moves between 1 and 6.
    • Moving up, down, left, or right requires 1 move.
    • Both players can jump over rocks; this requires 2 moves per rock.
    • Multiple rocks can be jumped over at a time, but only if the player has enought moves (e.g 4 moves are required to jump over two consecutive rocks).
    • All of the moves must be used.
    • Control switches to the other player when the current player runs out of moves.
    • As the Hunted could win by simply running down the clock, control switches to the Monster if they remain inactive for 5 seconds.
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
    • The Hunted should utilize the 5 seconds they have between moves to run down the clock.
    • The Monster should move as quickly as possible to make the most out of the time they have.
    • Jumping over rocks can be a very effective way of catching up with the Hunted or evading the Monster.
