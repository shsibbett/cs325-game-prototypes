Zombacalypse

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    • This is a single-player, post-apocalyptic strategy game where the player must utilize their hours to boost their defenses before the zombie horde attacks each night.
    • The player must clear shrubs surrounding building before they are allowed to repair them.
    • Each building has a different effect to help combat the zombies.
    • The player must also remove corpses from their base to reduce the number of zombies in the horde increasing their chances of survival.
    • The number of survivors lost depends on the number of zombies that attack.
    • The potential size of the horde increases by 5 zombies every 3 turns.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    • The prototype displays all the information the player could need to play the game.
    • The player uses the mouse to play, except when they are moving corpses which utilizes the cursor keys.
    • Each 'wrecked' and 'repaired' version of each building were stored in two different layers in the tilemap. When a building is repaired, the 'wrecked' building layer is destroyed, and the 'repaired' building layer is created.
    • The shrubs and corpses are a part of a 'shrubs' and 'corpses' group respectively. Each item in the groups was given an on click effect to remove shrubs/move corpses.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    • I learned how to make a game that uses the mouse instead of WASD or just the cursor keys.
    • I also learned how to destroy tilemap layers
    • I learned how to use groups in Phaser.
    • The easiest part was creating the groups for the shrubs and corpses.
    • It was also easy to destroy old tilemap layers and create new news to simulate a building being repaired.
    • The hardest part was thinking about how to do the corpse movement. Once it occured to me to use the cursor keys and a boolean to determine when the player could move a corpse, it wasn't that difficult.

  • Mandated Variety: State which input, randomness, genre, and play style you used in this prototype.
    • The player removes shrubs, repairs buildings, and chooses corpses to move with the mouse while corpses are moved with the cursor keys.
    • An element of randomness is the location of the corpses in the world map, the number of hours the player gets each turn (random number between 7-12), and the number of zombies that could attack (random number between 25-45 to start that increases by 5 every 3 turns).
    • The genre is post-apocalyptic strategy.
    • The playstyle is that the players must utilize their hours efficiently to be able to survive long enough to repair the cell block building.

  • Gameplay: A paragraph describing the actions the player can perform, the system dynamics, and the core mechanic. Include a concise explanation of the prototype’s inputs and their expected effects (how to play). You can also describe game play that is not in the prototype. You may include mock-up images for parts of the game not in the prototype.
    • This game is single-player.
    • It is the player's goal to repair the cell block facility before they lose all of their suvivors and are eaten by the horde.
    • Each turn, the player has a limited number of hours in which to clear overgrowth, repair buildings, or remove corpses (random value between 7-12).
    • After the player has run out of hours, a random number of zombies (starting with 25-45 zombies) will attack the survivors.
    • The number of survivors lost depends on the number of zombies and the current number of survivors that you have.
    • The minimum and maximum zombies in a horde increases by 5 zombies every 3 turns.
    • To survive, the player must repair facilities; these can only be repaired once all of the surrounding overgrowth has been cleared.
    • Clearing overgrowth cost 3 hours per shrub; the player clicks on a shrub to remove it.
    • Removing corpses from the prison yard permanently decreases the number of zombies in the horde by 1 for each corpse removed.
    • To remove a corpse, click on the corpse you wish to remove and use the cursor keys to move it to the edge of the map in any direction.
    • An indicator appears in the top right of the screen when you are in 'moving corpse' mode.
    • It costs 1 hour to move a corpse per 1 square of the map.
    • To deselect a corpse/get out of 'moving corpse' mode, press the enter key.
    • There are six facilities to repair; certain facilities take more hours to repair than others, and each has a different effect that is recieved upon repair.
    • Mouse over the buildings to see the description of the building, the cost of repair, the residual number of shrubs that need to be removed before repair, and the effect of repair.
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
    • The absolute first facility that should be acquired is the guard tower as it gives the player the 'guard' ability which lowers the number of casualties by 1 if the player 'guarded' before a zombie attack.
    • It is also imperative that the broadcast station be repaired ASAP to get the full benefits of its effect (obtain one new survivor every turn).
    • Removing corpses is a good way to increase your odds against the zombie horde as one zombie is removed from the horde for every corpse that you remove from the map.
    • Knowing when to repair the food storage building is important as it gives you a random number of new survivors and should only be used when the player is starting to run low on people.

Warnings of cannabalism and the undead rising fell deaf upon your ears, and you ignored the reports in favor of planning a trip to the Bahamas during your scheduled leave. The night before you and your family are to depart, you awake to the sounds of tearing flesh and the screams of your children in their bedroom. You are a drifter now and have stumbled across a group of people who are completely incapable of dealing with this crisis on their own. You decide to help them and, in time, have become their leader. Months have passed, and it has gotten harder and harder to find decent shelter from the relentless army of the undead. Fortunately for you, one of your survivors has stumbled across an abandoned prison facility untouched by the undead plague. You resolve to fortify it in the hopes that it can become a suitable home for you and your group.
